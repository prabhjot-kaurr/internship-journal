First Week — Hitting the Ground Running
May 11 – 16, 2026  ·  Mon – Sat
Done ✓ ▾
Monday · May 11, 2026  ·  Week 1, Day 1  ·  First Day ✦
📚 What I Learned
A presentation is not just slides — it's how you earn trust from day one. I learned that walking into a first meeting with research done speaks louder than anything you say. Also got my first real look at Google Search Console and what "organic visibility" actually means in practice.
⚠️ Mistakes & Fixes
Nervous energy made me rush through parts of the presentation. Lesson: slow down, let the work speak.
💼 Projects I Worked On
Presented my research on the company and shared ideas on what I could contribute. Received my first task: build a blog tracker to manage and measure the blogs I'd be working on. Built it the same day — with auto-updating numeric values so progress is always visible at a glance.
🔧 Tools & Skills Used
Google Search Console  ·  Google Sheets (advanced formulas & automation)  ·  Research & presentation skills
Tuesday · May 12, 2026  ·  Week 1, Day 2
📚 What I Learned
SEO is not a one-time fix — it's a full audit, rewrite, and strategy. Learned how to evaluate an existing blog's performance: structure, keyword use, meta details, and readability. Also learned what makes a backlink genuinely valuable — it's not about quantity, it's about relevance and trust.
⚠️ Mistakes & Fixes
Realised mid-way through the blog audit that I'd been skimming rather than reading deeply. Slowed down and the quality of my edits improved significantly.
💼 Projects I Worked On
Did a full SEO audit and content rewrite of an existing company blog — it went live on the website the same day. Built a backlink tracking sheet to manage outreach targets and log organic backlinks acquired. Secured 7 organic backlinks by end of day.
🔧 Tools & Skills Used
SEO content writing  ·  On-page SEO  ·  Backlink strategy  ·  Google Sheets  ·  WordPress
Wednesday · May 13, 2026  ·  Week 1, Day 3
📚 What I Learned
Guest posting is an art — finding the right platforms requires research, not just Google. Learned the difference between sites that accept genuine contributions vs. link farms. Also got hands-on with WordPress: uploading blogs, formatting, categories, and the small technical things that make a post publish-ready.
⚠️ Mistakes & Fixes
My first guest posting list included some low-authority sites. Went back and filtered by domain relevance and audience fit.
💼 Projects I Worked On
Researched and shortlisted guest posting opportunities. Wrote and uploaded a second fully SEO-optimised blog to the company website. Wrote an article specifically for backlink acquisition — targeting relevant external platforms.
🔧 Tools & Skills Used
WordPress (publishing & formatting)  ·  Guest post research  ·  Article writing for backlinks  ·  SEO writing
Thursday · May 14, 2026  ·  Week 1, Day 4
📚 What I Learned
Google Search Console is a goldmine if you know what to look for. Learned to read the difference between impressions, clicks, CTR, and average position — and how each metric tells a different story. The most exciting moment: seeing that a blog we'd updated was already gaining traction. Numbers don't lie.
⚠️ Mistakes & Fixes
Was trying to analyse everything at once. Learned to filter by query type and date range to draw cleaner insights.
💼 Projects I Worked On
Deep-dived into Google Search Console — analysed old blog performance, queries, keywords, CTR, positions, and clicks. Built a filtering sheet to identify which old blogs were worth updating vs. letting go. Wrote another SEO-optimised blog rewrite based on what the data showed.
🔧 Tools & Skills Used
Google Search Console (advanced analysis)  ·  Keyword research  ·  Data filtering  ·  Google Sheets  ·  SEO writing
Friday · May 15, 2026  ·  Week 1, Day 5
📚 What I Learned
My first real challenge — and honestly the most memorable learning of the week. The question: why weren't backlinks showing up in Google Search Console? I dug into it properly instead of guessing. The answer: GSC takes time to crawl new backlinks, and when the linking account is new, that delay is even longer. Patience is part of the strategy.
⚠️ Mistakes & Fixes
Initially assumed something was broken. Learned to investigate before concluding. Cross-referenced multiple platforms before landing on the right answer — that process itself was the lesson.
💼 Projects I Worked On
Built a backlink acquisition sheet listing potential sites to target for newly updated blogs. Worked on adding backlinks to recently published content. Researched and resolved the GSC backlink visibility question for the founder. Updated another old blog with fresh SEO content.
🔧 Tools & Skills Used
Google Search Console (troubleshooting)  ·  Backlink research  ·  Cross-platform analysis  ·  SEO writing
Saturday · May 16, 2026  ·  Week 1, Day 6
📚 What I Learned
Consistency compounds. Updating blogs isn't about writing something new every time — it's about finding what already has momentum and making it better. A blog with good impressions just needs a sharper rewrite to convert those views into clicks.
⚠️ Mistakes & Fixes
No major mistakes today — just refinement. Focused on quality over speed after a long week.
💼 Projects I Worked On
Identified a high-impression blog from the GSC data and gave it a full SEO content refresh. Published the updated version. Wrapped up the week by reviewing what had moved and what hadn't yet.
🔧 Tools & Skills Used
Google Search Console  ·  SEO writing  ·  WordPress  ·  Content strategy
Week 1 · Key Win
By the end of the week, impressions and clicks on updated blogs were already growing. Six days in, the work was showing results — and that was enough to know we were on the right track.

Building momentum and taking full ownership of the content pipeline — from writing and auditing, to publishing and indexing blogs on WordPress independently. Also created a structured guest posting strategy document and worked on audio-integrated content for the first time.
📚 Key Things I Learned
  • Working with audio content was completely new territory — generating, editing, and integrating audio into a blog changes how readers engage with it. I figured it out on the job.
  • Guest posting is not just about finding websites — it's about competitive analysis, filtering by domain authority, understanding the audience fit, and crafting a pitch that sounds human, not templated.
  • Getting WordPress access wasn't just a task — it was a responsibility. Learned how to publish, format, categorise, update HTML directly, and request indexing through Google Search Console, all in one week.
  • Content analysis through GSC data is only useful if you act on it. I built a prioritisation sheet to identify which old blogs deserved attention — and which ones to let go.

  • Analysed updated blogs from Week 1 to review performance and improvement areas

  • Wrote backlink articles for 2 updated blogs and completed all backlink tasks
  • Got full WordPress access — spent a day understanding the full system, updated an HTML file within WordPress
  • Built a prioritisation sheet identifying the most-viewed old blogs worth refreshing vs. those to archive

  • Drafted backlink articles for these new blog contents

WordPress was new to me, and on day one of access, there was a learning curve — understanding how content gets formatted, categories, HTML editing, and the publishing workflow. I didn't rush through it. I spent the day learning it properly, so that by the end of the week, I was publishing independently without needing guidance. The challenge became the foundation.

Week 2 · Biggest Win
Publishing my first blog completely independently — writing, uploading, formatting, indexing, and building backlinks for it, all on my own. Nobody hand-held me through it. Week 2 is when I stopped being a learner and started being a contributor.

Week three was the most intellectually demanding week so far. I published 2 original blogs under my own name, spotted a drop in GSC impressions before anyone else did, built a full SEO + GEO strategy report in response, and had it praised by the founder. This was the week I stopped executing and started thinking.

1SEO+GEO Strategy Report
🎯 What I Focused On This Week
Original content creation with a new, deeper strategy — incorporating real citations, university research, case studies, and E-E-A-T principles. Alongside that, proactively identifying a performance issue in GSC, investigating the root cause (including Google's new AI review box rollout), and building a strategy document that shifted the entire content direction of the company.
📚 Key Things I Learned
  • Generic SEO content is becoming invisible. When Google's AI can answer a question directly in the search results, your blog needs to offer something AI can't — real stories, original research, citations, case studies, and genuine human perspective. This is E-E-A-T in practice.
  • GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) isn't a future concept — it's already affecting traffic today. My certification in GEO gave me a framework to analyse what was happening and build a response strategy quickly.
  • A great report isn't just data — it's communication. I wrote two versions: a detailed SEO report and a simplified summary version for non-SEO readers. The founder read both. The summary version is what got the response.
  • Real stories make content rank and resonate. The blog I wrote about an NRI child learning Sanskrit mantras after learning Hindi — that story came from research and genuine case study work, not from a content brief. That's what makes a blog different.
💼 Projects & Tasks Completed
  • Uploaded and indexed 2 blogs, created backlinks for 2 blogs (Monday)
  • Published a new original blog with full content strategy — my name on it — with citations from top universities, real case studies of Hindustani Tongue's tutored kids, GEO and E-E-A-T applied throughout: How to Teach Kids Mother Tongue Abroad ↗
  • Requested indexing and created backlinks for this blog
  • On spotting a declining impressions trend in GSC, proactively contacted the founder, began independent research — cross-referencing Google's AI review box rollout with traffic data — and built a full SEO + GEO content strategy report with a non-technical summary version
  • Founder's response: the report was great — he specifically praised the summary version and immediately assigned follow-up GEO research
  • Conducted GEO research, shared findings with the founder, had a strategy meeting to redefine the entire content approach going forward
  • Was assigned an old blog with high title relevance — chose it strategically, linked a YouTube video within it, and created the first content draft under the new strategy
  • Finalised blog content incorporating a real story of a child who learned Hindi and then Sanskrit mantras — with citations, original research, and case studies. Got founder approval.
  • Published the finalised blog under my name, requested indexing, drafted backlink articles: Why NRIs Need to Know Their Mother Tongue ↗
⚠️ Challenges & How I Handled Them
The most significant challenge of the internship so far: noticing that GSC impressions were quietly dropping — and not panicking, but investigating. I didn't wait to be told. I researched what was happening, connected it to Google's AI review box update, and came back with a solution instead of just a problem. That instinct — to find the answer, not just flag the issue — is something I'm proud of. It changed the content strategy of the entire company.
🔧 Tools Used
Google Search Console (trend analysis & indexing)  ·  WordPress (publishing, formatting, YouTube embed)  ·  Google Sheets  ·  SEO + GEO strategy frameworks  ·  E-E-A-T content principles  ·  Citation & research sourcing  ·  Backlink strategy
Week 3 · Biggest Win
Two original blogs published under my own name — with real research, real stories, and a new content strategy I helped create. And the report that changed how the company thinks about SEO content. This was the week I went from executing tasks to influencing strategy.
